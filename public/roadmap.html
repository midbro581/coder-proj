Which track should I pick?

Real Talk

Advice Nobody Tells You

One language, one year

Don't switch languages every month. Every language looks interesting when you're a beginner. Pick one and get genuinely good at it first. The concepts transfer. The time doesn't.

Build things, even broken ones

Tutorials give you comfort. Projects give you skill. After every phase of this roadmap, build something with those skills — a to-do app, a calculator, a tiny game. It doesn't matter if it's ugly.

Google is not cheating

Every developer, every day, searches Stack Overflow, MDN, and GitHub. The skill is knowing what to search for. You're not supposed to memorize everything. Use the documentation.

It takes longer than you think

People say "learn to code in 3 months." That's marketing. Getting your first job realistically takes 1–2 years of consistent effort. That's not discouraging — it's just reality. Start now, keep going.
